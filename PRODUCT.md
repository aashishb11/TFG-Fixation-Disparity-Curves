# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Clinicians and researchers in optometry and vision science. They use this tool during patient assessments and academic research to fit fixation disparity curves and classify binocular vision behavior. Workflow: select a viewing distance, enter seven measured fixation disparity values at fixed vergence positions, run the statistical fit, review the resulting curves and classification, and export a clinical chart or report.

## Product Purpose

A precision tool for fitting and classifying fixation disparity curves (FDC) from clinical measurements. The tool runs four parametric models (Types I through IV) against the measured data, selects the best fit by SSE, and reports curve type classification, slope at the origin, fixation disparity at x = 0, and associated phoria. Results export as PNG chart or PDF clinical report.

## Brand Personality

Precise, composed, trustworthy. The interface is the researcher's instrument panel: uncluttered, readable, and authoritative. Nothing decorative earns its place without also serving the data or the workflow.

## Anti-references

- Generic SaaS dashboards with rounded card grids and glassmorphism
- AI tool dark-mode UIs with glowing gradient accents and gradient text
- Landing-page hero sections repurposed inside a research tool
- Floating rounded cards nested inside other floating rounded cards
- Decorative top-accent stripes, radial gradient blobs, and backdrop-blur effects

## Design Principles

1. Data over decoration. Every visual element either helps the user read data or navigate the workflow. Decoration without function is removed.
2. Structural clarity. Sections are defined by layout, spacing, and thin dividers, not by floating cards with shadows.
3. One surface level. Content panels sit flat on the page. Nesting cards adds visual noise without adding information.
4. Typography carries hierarchy. Scale and weight contrast distinguish section labels from values. Uppercase tracking is reserved for data-table column headers only.
5. The chart is primary. All supporting content frames the chart. The chart is simply and clearly presented, not wrapped in decorative chrome.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG 2.1 AA target. Keyboard navigable. Visible focus states on all interactive elements. Semantic HTML throughout. Accessible labels on all form controls. Error states communicated in text, not color alone.
